STORY

The Story of Koenigsegg

Koenigsegg Automotive AB was founded in 1994 by Christian von Koenigsegg, a young Swedish entrepreneur with a passion for cars and innovation. From a very early age, Christian dreamed of creating the ultimate supercar—one that would combine cutting-edge technology, extreme performance, and bespoke craftsmanship. At just 22 years old, Christian used the funds from a successful trading business to launch his own car company. Inspired by the success of brands like Ferrari and McLaren, he aimed to build a car that would rival the best in the world. He established the company in Ängelholm, Sweden. The first prototype, the Koenigsegg CC, was completed in 1996. It featured a lightweight carbon fiber body and a unique detachable hardtop that could be stored in the trunk—an innovation at the time. After years of development, the production version, the CC8S, was released in 2002 and gained international attention, even setting a Guinness World Record for the most powerful production engine. From there, Koenigsegg continued to innovate, introducing technologies like the Triplex suspension, Freevalve camless engine system, and advanced carbon fiber construction. Their cars, such as the Agera RS, Regera, and Jesko, have broken numerous performance records and challenged the limits of automotive engineering. Today, Koenigsegg is recognized as one of the most advanced and exclusive hypercar manufacturers in the world, known for pushing the boundaries of what a car can do.
